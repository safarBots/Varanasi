Best Places To Visit in Varanasi

Sarnath Museum

The Sarnath Museum is the oldest site museum of the Archaeological Survey of India, located near the famous Sarnath archaeological site, where Buddha delivered his first sermon. The museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts, including the famous Lion Capital of Ashoka, which is now the national emblem of India. The museum’s collection showcases Buddhist sculptures, inscriptions, and other relics from the Mauryan, Kushana, and Gupta periods.

Ramnagar Fort:

Located on the eastern bank of the Ganges River, Ramnagar Fort was built in the 18th century by Maharaja Balwant Singh. The fort serves as the residence of the Kashi Naresh (the king of Varanasi) and is a fine example of Mughal-style architecture. Inside the fort, you’ll find the Ramnagar Fort Museum, which displays a variety of historical artifacts, including vintage cars, weapons, medieval costumes, and manuscripts. The fort is also known for its association with the grand Ram Lila celebrations during Dussehra.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Kashi Vishwanath Temple, also known as Vishwanath Mandir, is one of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in the heart of Varanasi, it is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest of Shiva temples. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times in history, with its current structure built by the Maratha ruler Ahilya Bai Holkar in 1780. The temple is an important pilgrimage site, attracting millions of devotees and tourists each year.

Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum

Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum, located within the Banaras Hindu University (BHU) campus, is a treasure trove of Indian art and culture. Established in 1920, the museum’s collection includes a wide range of artifacts, such as paintings, sculptures, textiles, coins, and rare manuscripts. The museum is particularly known for its collection of miniature paintings, Rajasthani and Mughal paintings, and archaeological materials, making it a must-visit for art and history enthusiasts.

Nepali Temple (Kathwala Temple)

The Nepali Temple, also known as Kathwala Temple, is a unique temple situated on the Lalita Ghat in Varanasi. It was built by the King of Nepal and is an excellent example of traditional Nepalese architecture, made of wood and featuring intricate carvings. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is often referred to as the ‘Mini Khajuraho’ due to its detailed woodwork. The serene location by the river Ganges and its distinct architectural style make it a peaceful retreat for visitors.
